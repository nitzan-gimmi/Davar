# Open Hebrew Standard Vision Document

## Introduction  
The Open Hebrew Standard (OHS) aims to redefine and revitalize the Hebrew language and its usage through a comprehensive framework that emphasizes linguistic sovereignty and cultural integrity.

## Anterpism Philosophy  
The philosophy of Anterpism serves as the backbone of the OHS, advocating for a proactive and constructive approach to language development. It encourages individuals and communities to contribute actively to the evolution of language, ensuring it reflects contemporary needs while respecting historical roots.

## Linguistic Sovereignty  
Linguistic sovereignty is a core principle of the OHS, emphasizing the right of speakers to control their own language, determine its evolution, and create guidelines that reflect their values and identity. This principle underlines the importance of community involvement in language preservation and innovation.

## Core Modules  
### 1. Accuracy/DIK  
Focusing on precision in language use, the Accuracy or DIK module provides tools and methodologies to ensure that language is used correctly and effectively in various contexts. This module emphasizes the importance of clarity and understanding in communication.

### 2. Resonance/KUM  
The Resonance or KUM module addresses the emotional and cultural impact of language. It aims to create a deep connection between speakers and their language, fostering pride and a sense of belonging within the community.

### 3. Purpose/IYL  
The Purpose or IYL module focuses on the significance and intent behind language usage. It encourages speakers to express their identity and values through language, ensuring that communication is meaningful and contextually relevant.

## HFBT Architecture  
The HFBT (Hebrew Functional Basis Technology) architecture serves as the technical framework for implementing the OHS. It integrates modern technology and linguistic theories to support a dynamic and adaptable linguistic ecosystem.

## Hippocratic License 3.0  
The OHS operates under the Hippocratic License 3.0, ensuring that all contributors adhere to ethical guidelines that prioritize linguistic and cultural integrity.

## Roadmap for Implementation  
1. **Community Engagement**: Foster a community of speakers and contributors who are committed to the principles of the OHS.  
2. **Development of Modules**: Create and refine the core modules with input from experts and users.  
3. **Technology Integration**: Develop tools and platforms that utilize the HFBT architecture to support language use and learning.  
4. **Continuous Feedback and Iteration**: Establish mechanisms for ongoing feedback from users to continuously refine and improve the OHS.

## Conclusion  
The Open Hebrew Standard is a visionary initiative designed to empower Hebrew speakers and ensure the language thrives in the modern world. By embracing Anterpism philosophy, upholding linguistic sovereignty, and implementing core modules, the OHS aims to create a sustainable linguistic future for the Hebrew language.
